About Dr. G. Sucharitha.

Professor, researcher, mentor. Building intelligent systems for medical and multimodal understanding — backed by 15 years of teaching and a portfolio of Q1 publications, patents and funded projects.

Biography

From signal processing to scientific machine learning.

Dr. G. Sucharitha is a Professor in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at Anurag University, Hyderabad. Her research sits at the intersection of computer vision, deep learning and biomedical imaging — building methods that move from algorithmic novelty to clinically and industrially deployable systems.

She holds a Ph.D. from KL University in computer-vision-related research, an M.Tech in Digital Systems & Computer Electronics from JNTUH, and a B.Tech in Electronics & Communication Engineering from JNTUH. Over fifteen years, her academic career has spanned faculty roles at IFHE, IARE and Ambala College of Engineering — most recently leading the Data Science vertical of CSE as Head of Department at the Institute of Aeronautical Engineering (Mar 2021 – Sep 2024).

Her work has appeared in Scientific Reports (Springer Nature), IEEE Access, Cluster Computing, Multimedia Tools and Applications, Egyptian Informatics Journal, the ACM JDIQ, and IEEE conferences including AVSS, ICOIN and ICCCNT — totalling 41+ publications, 13 book chapters, two edited books and a Google Scholar h-index of 12 (457+ citations).

Research Philosophy

Curiosity-driven, translation-minded.

"The most useful AI research starts with a real problem — a clinician's question, an industry pain point, a dataset that refuses to behave — and ends with something deployable, interpretable and reproducible."

My approach is grounded in three commitments: scientific rigor (novel formulations, careful baselines, peer-reviewed venues), translational intent (medical imaging, edge intelligence, blockchain-backed health data), and open mentorship (guiding PhD scholars and undergraduates through their first conference papers, then their first Q1 journals).

I work most productively in cross-disciplinary teams — pairing vision/ML expertise with domain partners in radiology, agronomy, banking and edge networking. If your problem fits that shape, I'd love to talk.

What I value

Operating principles

  • Strong methodological novelty before benchmark chasing.
  • Reproducible pipelines and well-documented experimental setups.
  • Patents and publications as complementary outputs.
  • Long-term student development over short-term metrics.
  • Honest peer review and open scientific discourse.
